# The Solar System

The Solar System formed roughly 4.6 billion years ago from a giant
interstellar cloud of gas and dust. At its center is the Sun, a G-type
main-sequence star that contains about 99.8% of the system's total mass.

## Planets

There are eight planets. The four inner, rocky planets are Mercury, Venus,
Earth, and Mars. The four outer planets are the gas giants Jupiter and Saturn,
and the ice giants Uranus and Neptune.

Jupiter is the largest planet in the Solar System. It is so massive that it is
more than twice as heavy as all the other planets combined. Saturn is famous for
its extensive and bright ring system, made mostly of ice particles.

Mars is often called the Red Planet because iron oxide on its surface gives it a
reddish appearance. Earth is the only planet currently known to support life.
